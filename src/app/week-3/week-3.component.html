Introduction

Week three of my On-the-Job Training (OJT) was both exciting and nerve-wracking. Our team had to present a new system we planned to develop during OJT. The challenge was to create something simple yet useful and challenge-friendly.

We decided on NearByTask, a mobile platform designed for TESDA graduates to find job opportunities by completing client-posted tasks. Our goal was to create a system that generates income, but it wasn’t an easy decision. The project involved multiple modules and features, and we were assigned to build it using Flutter (for mobile) and Angular (for the admin panel)—two technologies we had no prior experience with.

This week, our main focus was to ensure the system’s feasibility and start coding the front-end.
